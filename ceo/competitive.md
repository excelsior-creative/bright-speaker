# Competitive

_Updated 2026-04-17. Keep short; update when the picture shifts._

## Adult-focused speaking coaches (indirect)

- **Yoodli** — AI feedback on filler words, pace, and content.
  Adult-facing, professional-interview positioning. Strong NLP.
  Not designed for K-12; privacy and tone are not kid-appropriate
  by default. Our wedge: they won't build for kids.
- **Orai** — Legacy mobile-first filler-word trainer. Gamification
  elements. Mobile-first, which is a miss for Chromebook-first
  K-12 schools.
- **Speeko** — Similar. Mobile, adult.
- **Poised** — Meeting-focused, real-time coaching during calls.
  Not our category.

## K-12 adjacent

- **Flip (formerly Flipgrid, Microsoft)** — Shut down July 2024.
  Was the dominant video-response tool in K-12. Left a real gap
  in classrooms. Teachers are looking for alternatives.
  **Opportunity**: Bright Speaker is not a Flip replacement
  (different category), but teachers searching for "Flipgrid
  alternative" is a high-intent corridor to monitor.
- **Padlet + Canva** — General-purpose; teachers improvise.
- **Speech-therapy apps** (e.g., Articulation Station) — Clinical,
  not general communication. Different buyer, regulated.
- **Toastmasters Pathways / Gavel Club** — Community-based, not
  software. Partner opportunity more than competition.

## Speech & debate software

- **Tabroom (NSDA)** — Tournament management, not practice.
- **Speechgeek / Forensics Tournament Services** — Events.
- **Debate Hackademy, SpeechwireSolo** — Niche tools.

No one owns "practice with AI feedback for speech & debate."
That's the wedge.

## Classroom speaking rubrics / curriculum

Free / cheap rubrics from teachers-pay-teachers, state ELA
standards, Common Core SL.1 – SL.6. Our blog content should meet
teachers where they're searching (SL standards, rubric templates).

## Where we win

- **Only product designed from the ground up for K-12 speech
  practice** with kid-appropriate feedback.
- **Browser-first, no install** — works on a Chromebook the
  student already has.
- **Privacy-first architecture** — student video never leaves
  the device.
- **Practice-rep model** — teachers don't have to grade
  anything new.

## Where we are at risk

- A well-funded adult-focused competitor (Yoodli) pivots down-
  market into K-12. Defense: K-12 branding, kid-appropriate
  tone, educator trust, and privacy story.
- A district-level ELA curriculum vendor (HMH, McGraw Hill,
  Savvas) adds a speaking module to an existing adoption.
  Defense: be better at the practice-rep experience by an
  order of magnitude and be the educator's preferred pick.

## What we need to watch

- Any "AI debate coach" press releases.
- NSDA partnerships with any software vendor.
- Google Workspace for Education announcements about built-in
  speaking practice.
- Any edtech M&A that pulls Flip-style video-response back
  into the market.
