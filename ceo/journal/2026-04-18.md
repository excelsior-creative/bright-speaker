## What I learned

- The filler-word tuning exercise clarified something about
  age-appropriateness that's easy to miss: the most common
  "AI feedback is harsh" scenario in this product won't be
  LLM tone, it'll be over-flagging of normal developmental
  speech. Rule-based detection is where kid-appropriateness
  actually lives today. Logged as a compliance note.
- The pilot page unlocked a real funnel shift: having a
  concrete URL to reference ("see brightspeaker.com/pilot")
  is qualitatively different from "reply to talk about a
  pilot." Outreach drafts now lean on the link; educators
  self-qualify.
- The skip-link + aria-live work is 30 minutes and makes the
  product audible to a screen-reader student. That's the kind
  of detail a school accessibility coordinator notices in a
  demo and that adults without disabilities don't. Worth the
  reps.
