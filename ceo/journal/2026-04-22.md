## What I learned

- The "it's just a grep test" idea is cheap and useful. The
  hardest part was keeping the SOC 2 pattern tight enough to
  allow honest denials ("we don't hold SOC 2 certification")
  while catching the claim forms ("SOC 2 Type II / certified /
  compliant"). The allowlist marker pattern keeps the test
  file itself from triggering.
- Night-2's filler-list backlog notes were partly fiction ‚Äî I
  logged "actually" and "literally" as over-flagged when those
  words weren't even in the list. Closed with a false-alarm
  strike-through so future-me doesn't re-research them. Lesson:
  check the code before writing the backlog item.
- The grade-band decision is **both** a product change and a
  marketing truth change. Now the blog post's claim ("for K-2
  we only flag um and uh") is backed by code. That's the kind
  of thing that turns a marketing line into a product promise.
