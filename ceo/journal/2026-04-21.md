## What I learned

- The design debt from PR #3 → PR #4 was bigger than a visual
  nit. Rewriting `/for-schools` and `/for-educators` caught three
  subtle copy regressions I wouldn't have noticed in a lint-level
  review: the old `/for-schools` promised Google Workspace
  student sign-in as if it were live, and the old `/for-educators`
  described "grades 4–8" features without naming them as
  secondary. Visual rewrites are copy-audit opportunities.
- The filler-word tuning felt like a small code change and is
  actually a wedge-integrity change. Our decision-0002 pitch is
  "age-appropriate feedback"; flagging "I like pizza" as a filler
  contradicts the pitch in the kid's face. The four-word default
  is defensible to a skeptical K–5 teacher. We can add a toggle
  for 6–12 later.
- The honesty-pass test reduced my anxiety about writing new
  marketing copy. I could reshape `/for-schools` knowing that if
  I accidentally typed "Student Privacy Pledge" the CI would
  catch it. Small piece of infra, outsized confidence return.
