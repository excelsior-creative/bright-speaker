# Draft — Charter network elementary curriculum director cold intro

**Recipient:** Director (or VP / Senior Director) of Elementary
Curriculum at a multi-school charter network (CMO) — KIPP, Success
Academy, Achievement First, Uncommon, IDEA, Aspire, BASIS, Rocketship,
Democracy Prep, Friendship, etc., or a regional CMO. Personalize per
recipient.

**Context / why now:** Charter networks are an underweighted lane for
us so far — every existing draft targets a single building or a
district. Networks are interesting because (a) they have a single
curriculum decision-maker who can run a pilot across multiple sites,
(b) they typically have unified Chromebook + Google Workspace for
Education stacks, (c) elementary literacy is on most CMO scorecards,
and (d) they tend to share a single ELA scope and sequence — making
oral-language alignment a genuinely cross-school conversation.

This draft is also positioned around end-of-year planning: late
April / early May is when curriculum directors are scoping summer
PD and fall pilots.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, operationally specific, no education jargon
that a curriculum director would roll their eyes at. We do NOT lead
with the Title I discount here — that is the elementary principal
draft's frame; for a CMO director, the lead is "you can run this in
three buildings with one teacher per site, with one decision."

**Status:** Draft. Brandon reviews, personalizes, sends.

---

**Subject line options:**

- Oral-language practice for K–5, in three of your schools
- A small ELA-aligned tool, designed for a CMO-style rollout
- 30 days, three classrooms, no cost: K–5 speaking practice

---

**Body:**

Hi [first name],

I'm [Brandon] at Bright Speaker. We make a browser-based speaking
practice tool for K–5 classrooms, mapped to Common Core SL.K–SL.5,
and I'm reaching out because [Network] is the kind of organization
where we'd actually learn something useful from a pilot — one
curriculum lead, three schools, three teachers, 30 days, no cost.

The reason I'm pitching a multi-site pilot rather than a single
classroom: oral language is the strand most CMOs tell us they want
to do better on, and the strand most often the first to get cut
when ELA blocks compress. A three-school pilot lets you compare
across school cultures and Chromebook deployments without taking
on a network-wide commitment.

What you'd actually get:

- **The product, free for 30 days.** Browser-based, runs on the
  Chromebooks your teachers already have. No installs, no new
  student accounts to manage if you use Google Workspace for
  Education.
- **20-minute kickoff with each teacher.** Live, with us. We do
  the lift, not your coaches.
- **A day-30 readout.** Practice completion rates by school, an
  honest "what did we see" summary, and three concrete things
  we'd change before a network-wide rollout.
- **Per-student feedback that is age-appropriate by design.**
  Filler word lists are tuned by grade band — K–2 is `um/uh`
  only; we are deliberately not flagging "like" or "you know"
  for a second grader. Feedback is about the speaking, not the
  speaker.

A few things we're up-front about:

- Student video never leaves the device. The only data we hold
  is transcripts and scores tied to a student account.
- We don't currently hold SOC 2, COPPA, or FERPA certifications.
  We can sign a DPA before any paid deployment, and we can walk
  through every sub-processor on a pilot kickoff call.
- Chrome's built-in Web Speech API does the transcription, which
  means audio routes through Google's speech servers during a
  session. We disclose this rather than paper over it; for some
  networks that is the deciding factor and we'd rather you know
  on day one.

**One ask:** would a 25-minute call work in the next two weeks to
talk through what a 30-day, three-school pilot would look like at
[Network]? If the answer is "not now," I'll send the SL.K–SL.5
classroom checklist we just published [link: /resources/sl-k-5-speaking-checklist]
and not follow up.

Thanks for the time.

[Brandon Johnson / Founder, Bright Speaker]
hello@brightspeaker.com · brightspeaker.com

**Internal notes for Brandon:**

- Don't blast this to ten CMOs at once. Two or three a week,
  personalized to whatever the network's most recent ELA strategy
  document says (most CMOs publish a public "academic vision" or
  "instructional priorities" deck). Reference it specifically in
  paragraph one.
- If the network is K–8 or K–12, the K–5 framing is still right;
  we explicitly do not sell as a 6–12 product yet.
- The three-school pilot is the differentiator vs. the principal
  draft. Don't water it down to "one classroom" unless they ask;
  CMOs respond to operational discipline.
- Networks with strong "no third-party data" stances (Success
  Academy historically, some classical-tradition networks) may
  hard-stop on the Web Speech API disclosure. Mention it in the
  email, don't try to bury it on a follow-up call.
- When they reply, ask for a 25-minute call AND for the names of
  one or two K–5 ELA leads at specific schools. Curriculum
  directors don't always own school-level scheduling.
- After 14 days no reply: do not bump. CMO directors filter bumps.
  Send the principal draft to a school-level leader at the same
  network instead.
