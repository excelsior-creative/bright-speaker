# Draft — Independent / private K–5 enrichment program director cold intro

**Recipient:** Director of enrichment, auxiliary programs, or
out-of-classroom learning at an independent, private, or charter K–5
school. Title varies — "Director of Auxiliary Programs," "Director
of Enrichment," "Dean of Student Life," "Director of Co-Curricular,"
occasionally "Director of Student Development."
**Context / why now:** The 30-day pilot page is now live at
`brightspeaker.com/pilot`. The K–5 positioning is in place across
the homepage, for-educators, and for-schools. This is the fourth
persona on our gtm.md ICP list (teacher, principal, literacy coach,
and now enrichment director) and the first outreach draft targeting
independent / private K–5 schools specifically.
**Why this persona:** Enrichment directors at independent K–5
schools have real budget autonomy (often $10–$25k/year for small
program adds), decision cycles measured in weeks not quarters,
direct line to the head of school, and strong interest in the "makes
us different" story that oral-language practice offers. They are
our fastest-signing lane.
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, specific, and a little playful. Independent
school leaders can sniff out boilerplate instantly; lean into
concrete details about their program. Not equity-framed (that's the
Title I draft's lane) — this one is about curriculum differentiation
and student confidence.
**Status:** Draft. Brandon reviews, personalizes per recipient,
sends.

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

I'm [Brandon / a colleague at Bright Speaker]. We're a small team
building a browser-based speaking-practice tool for K–5 classrooms,
and I'm writing because [School]'s [public-speaking program / oral
language focus / Harkness-style discussions / specific detail from
their site] is the kind of environment where what we build actually
lands.

The short pitch:

- **What it is.** A 60-second speaking warm-up a student runs on a
  Chromebook or iPad. Prompts map to Common Core SL.K–SL.5. Kids get
  real-time feedback on eye contact, pace, and filler words — tuned
  for elementary developmental stage, not adult speech coaching.
- **How it fits your day.** Designed for a five-minute slot before
  literacy block. No new accounts, no install, no curriculum
  re-write. Teachers assign a prompt; kids speak; teachers see a
  simple picture of who's practicing.
- **Privacy by default.** Student video never leaves the device. No
  ad tracking, no data brokering. We'll sign a DPA on request and
  we'll tell you directly that we don't currently hold SOC 2 /
  COPPA / FERPA certifications — we'd rather be honest than papier-
  mâché your due-diligence process.

**One ask:** we're running free 30-day classroom pilots this spring
and fall. Would it be worth a 20-minute call to walk one of your
lower-school teachers through what that looks like? The details are
at [brightspeaker.com/pilot]. If yes, I'll send three time options;
if no, I'll let this sit — no follow-ups.

Either way, here's a standards-aligned K–5 warm-up routine we just
published, ready to print:

And a longer piece on the year-at-a-glance scope we think actually
works:

[link: brightspeaker.com/blog/how-to-teach-public-speaking-elementary-school]

Thanks for the time.

[Brandon Johnson / Founder, Bright Speaker]

**Internal notes for Brandon:**

- Independent schools respond to specificity. Spend 2 minutes on the
  school's website looking for: any speech/debate club, any
  Harkness / seminar-style program, any lower-school oral language
  initiative, any "confident communicators" phrasing in their
  mission. Name it in paragraph one.
- Do not call it a "discount" to this audience. Independent schools
  aren't shopping on price — frame the pilot as a program add with
  no budget cost during evaluation.
- If the recipient's title is "Dean of Student Life" or similar
  (not enrichment / curriculum), swap the first bullet for something
  about low-stakes practice for shy or developing speakers. That
  resonates more with dean-of-student-life portfolios than the
  curriculum angle.
- If the school has a notable speech and debate program at the
  middle or upper level, mention it once and link our middle-school
  content (`/for-schools` for now; we may want a grades 6–12
  crosswalk page later if the independent lane heats up).
- When they reply, pivot immediately to a calendar link — don't
  email-ping-pong to schedule. Independent school leaders are
  calendar-full.
- Don't claim COPPA/FERPA-compliant. The "we'd rather be honest"
  line is one of our strongest trust signals with this audience —
  lean into it.
