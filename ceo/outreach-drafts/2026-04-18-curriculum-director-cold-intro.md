## Notes for Brandon

- This is a deliberately longer, more direct email than the
  teacher version. Administrators trust specifics; they delete
  anything that reads like a sales template.
- The bullet list is 3 items, not 5. Any more and you lose them.
- The compliance paragraph is load-bearing. Do NOT trim it. A
  C&I director who hears &quot;COPPA-compliant&quot; from a
  pre-launch vendor assumes it&apos;s a lie.
- &quot;Reply with windows&quot; is intentional — makes the next
  step concrete and low-friction. Don&apos;t switch to
  &quot;click here to book a time&quot; unless you hear back.
- Personalize line 1: reference a recent district announcement,
  strategic plan goal with the word &quot;communication,&quot;
  or a school-board agenda where oral communication came up.
- The P.S. is the second highest-read line after subject. Use it
  for the pilot URL, not a pleasantry.
- If they ask about comparison to Orai/Speeko/Yoodli: we&apos;re
  purpose-built for K-12 — kid-appropriate feedback, school-
  rostered, no consumer upsell. Those tools target adults and
  carry consumer-flavored data flows. Don&apos;t lead with this;
  only if asked.
