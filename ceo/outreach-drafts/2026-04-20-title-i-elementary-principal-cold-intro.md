# Draft — Title I elementary principal cold intro

**Recipient:** Principal of a Title I-designated elementary school
(K–5 or PreK–5). Personalize per recipient.
**Context / why now:** The homepage now leads with K–5 elementary
positioning (ages 5–11, Common Core SL.K–SL.5) and offers a 50%
Title I discount. Elementary principals are our new primary ICP
after decision 0002. This is the first cold draft targeting them.
**Tone:** Warm, credible, equity-framed but not performative. Short.
Assume the principal is triaging a full inbox in the 20 minutes
before walk-through. One ask.
**Status:** Draft. Brandon reviews, personalizes, and sends.

---

**Subject line options (pick one per recipient):**

- Oral-language practice for every 3rd grader — free pilot for [School]
- Five-minute daily speaking practice, built for Title I elementary
- A small tool for a big SL standard

---

**Body:**

Hi [Principal last name],

I'm [Brandon / a colleague at Bright Speaker]. We're a small team
building a speaking-practice tool designed specifically for K–5
classrooms, and I'm reaching out because [School] is exactly the kind
of community we'd like to support with a free pilot this spring.

Here's the short version:

- **The product** is a browser-based warm-up. Students open a class
  code on a Chromebook, get a 60-second speaking prompt aligned to
  Common Core SL.K–SL.5, and receive age-appropriate feedback on
  eye contact, pace, and filler words. Teachers see a simple
  per-student picture of who's practicing and where they're
  growing.
- **Privacy is the default.** Student video never leaves the device.
  No ad tracking, no data brokering, no third-party analytics.
  Chrome's built-in Web Speech API handles transcription — we
  disclose that plainly rather than paper over it.
- **Title I schools are our first focus.** Oral-language
  instructional time is under-resourced almost everywhere, and
  especially where it matters most. Our Title I pricing is 50% off
  per-student, per-year across the board — for pilots there's no
  cost at all.

**One ask:** would you be open to a 20-minute call where I walk one
of your K–5 teachers through what a free 30-day classroom pilot
would look like? If yes, I'll follow up with two or three time
options. If no, I'll let this sit — no follow-ups.

Either way, here's the short K–5 warm-up routine we're publishing
this week, ready to print and use tomorrow:

[link: /blog/five-minute-elementary-speaking-warmup]

Thanks for the time, and thank you for the work.

[Brandon Johnson / Founder, Bright Speaker]
hello@brightspeaker.com · brightspeaker.com

**Internal notes for Brandon:**

- Send one per day, not a blast. Title I principals field a lot of
  vendor mail; a personalized note is the only version that works.
- Research hook before sending: 30 seconds on the school's site to
  find a specific oral-language or literacy initiative, then tweak
  paragraph one to name it. Principals can tell the difference.
- If you know the principal personally or have a mutual, use a
  warm-intro variant instead of this cold draft.
- Don't claim COPPA/FERPA-compliant. "Privacy is the default" with
  specifics is the right line.
- When they reply, pivot immediately to a calendar link — don't
  email-ping-pong to schedule.
