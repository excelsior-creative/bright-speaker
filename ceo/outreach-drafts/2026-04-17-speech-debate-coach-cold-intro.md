**Subject:** Quick speech & debate question

Hi [First name],

I'm Brandon — I'm building Bright Speaker
(brightspeaker.com), a browser-based practice tool for K-12
speakers. It's designed for the reality of coaching: you can't
run 1:1 drills with 30 kids, and structured reps are what
actually moves a speaker.

Students open it in Chrome on their Chromebook, pick a 60-second
prompt, and speak to the camera. The tool gives them feedback on
filler words, pacing, and eye contact in real time. No video
ever leaves their device — this matters to me personally, because
it's a kids' product first.

I'm looking for 3 coaches who'd help me shape what's useful for
actual teams, starting this semester. No cost for a 30-day pilot,
no install, no accounts for you to manage if your school uses
Google Workspace for Education.

Worth a 15-minute call? I'll show you the flow and ask what's
actually missing.

— Brandon Johnson
   Founder, Bright Speaker
   brightspeaker.com

## Notes for Brandon

- Personalize line 1 with something specific about the coach's
  program (a tournament placement, a recent post, a specific
  curriculum piece). Cold-without-personalization rarely lands
  with coaches.
- Do NOT bcc-blast this. One at a time, personalized.
- If they reply positively, the next email points them at the
  pilot one-pager (`2026-04-17-pilot-program-one-pager.md`) and
  offers a Calendly link.
- If they ask "is this COPPA-compliant?" — do NOT claim
  compliance. Say "we're privacy-first by architecture —
  student video never leaves the device — and we're working on
  formal compliance review ahead of paid pilots. Happy to walk
  through our data flows on a call."
