**Subject:** A question about your oral presentation unit

Hi [First name],

I'm Brandon, building Bright Speaker
(brightspeaker.com). It's a browser-based speaking practice
platform designed for K-12 — students open it on their
Chromebook, pick a 60-second prompt, and speak to the camera.
The tool catches filler words, tracks pacing, and checks eye
contact, then shows them a kid-appropriate summary.

The thing I kept hearing from ELA teachers while I was building
it: "I love oral presentations but I can't grade another video
pile." Bright Speaker isn't another thing to grade. It gives
students the practice reps that make the real presentation
better, and it gives you a classroom view of who's actually
practicing and where they're stuck.

I'd love to put it in your classroom free this semester. No
install, and if your district uses Google Workspace for
Education, students sign in with their school account.

Worth a reply? I can send a 2-minute walkthrough video, or
pre-set up a class code for you to try.

— Brandon Johnson
   Founder, Bright Speaker
   brightspeaker.com

P.S. Student video never leaves the device. That was the first
design constraint — not an afterthought.
