# GTM

_Target customers, pricing, pipeline, pitch state._

## Ideal customer profile (working hypothesis)

**Primary buyer (wedge)** — Individual educator with spending
autonomy and a personal stake in communication outcomes:

- Middle / high school speech & debate coach (6–12).
- ELA teacher grades 4–8 who runs an oral presentation unit or
  Socratic seminar.
- Enrichment program director at a private or independent school.

**Expansion buyer (after the wedge takes hold)** — Building-level
or district curriculum/instructional technology decision-maker:

- Curriculum & Instruction director for ELA or literacy.
- Instructional Technology coordinator evaluating communication
  tools.
- Assistant Superintendent for Teaching & Learning.

**School profile we're targeting first** — Suburban or independent,
1:1 device programs (usually Chromebook), with an existing speech &
debate program or a teacher champion who already runs oral
practice. Avoid the largest urban districts until we have a case
study; their procurement cycles are multi-quarter.

## Positioning — one sentence

> Bright Speaker gives every K-12 student the reps they need to
> become a confident speaker, without adding a minute to a teacher's
> day.

## Key messages for educators

1. **Reps, not recordings.** Practice is the thing; we're not
   asking teachers to grade another video pile.
2. **Kid-appropriate feedback.** Every tip is about the speaking,
   not the speaker. Warm by design.
3. **Works on a Chromebook.** No installs, no new accounts to
   manage if you use Google Workspace for Education.
4. **Private by design.** Student video never leaves the device.
   Transcripts and scores are the only data stored.
5. **Costs less than a pizza party.** Per-student pricing designed
   for building budgets, not enterprise procurement.

## Pricing (DRAFT — not yet tested with educators)

- **Teacher Free** — One classroom, up to ~35 students. Core
  practice loop, basic teacher view. Indefinite. Our funnel.
- **School** — ~$3–$6 per student / year. Adds class assignment,
  progress analytics, admin seat, CSV exports.
- **District** — Custom. Adds SSO with Google Workspace for
  Education, DPA signing, data residency discussion, roster
  provisioning, priority support.

**Open question**: one-time vs. annual. Schools strongly prefer
annual line items in a budget. Committing to annual.

**Open question**: pilot pricing. Proposal: 30-day pilots are
free; formal pilot letter required from the school; explicit
success criteria at the start (we pick 3 KRs with the teacher).

## Pilot program design (draft)

- 30 days, one classroom, one teacher.
- We provide a 20-minute onboarding call with the teacher.
- Teacher commits to assigning 2 practice sessions / week.
- At day 30, we sit down with the teacher for 20 minutes and review:
  - Session completion rate.
  - Filler-word reduction between first and last session (per
    student).
  - Teacher-reported confidence delta (5-question survey pre/post).
- We ask for permission to write a case study. We will anonymize
  student data unless we get written consent otherwise.
